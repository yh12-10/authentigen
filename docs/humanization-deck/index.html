Honest conclusions

Key findings

Portraits
Heavy

Grain + warmth flatter skin; no crisp edges to fringe. The 35 mm profile is made for faces.

Product shots
Medium

Believable lens character without over-texturing — Heavy's aberration degrades crisp label type.

Landscapes
Heavy

Big organic gradients take heavy grain and sun bloom well, for the most photographic look.

Limitations — stated plainly

  • Structural AI tells are out of scope. Perfect mirror reflections (the marble & lake), anatomy and geometry are untouched — this is surface camera character, not regeneration.
  • Aberration is eased on skin (20%), so it won't "fix" flawed skin tone or blending.
  • Shadow-chroma, haze & neon effects only fire on dark scenes — bright daylight subjects get lighter treatment by design.
  • The vignette is fixed-position and grain is a global overlay — neither adds true local detail.

Pipeline verdict

It does what it claims — and is honest about what it can't.

The humanizer reliably adds believable camera & film character across portraits, products and landscapes, with a clear best setting per subject. It makes no claim to repair structural AI artifacts, and the limitations are documented. Good to ship.
